abstract: |
  Long-form writing for online literature demands immense creative effort, yet authors are often bogged down by the high cognitive load of maintaining narrative consistency across millions of words.

  To address this, we started a 2-stage formative study with professional authors, deriving 3 design goals, motivating a “Write, Audit, Correct” workflow for author-controlled human-AI collaboration. Based on this, we present TRACE, a system leveraging dynamic structured memory to check for narrative inconsistencies following the cognitive writing process.

  A user study with 12 authors provides early evidence that TRACE helps offload the cognitive burden of consistency checking, enabling them to remain focused and maintain their creative flow. Our work offers design implications for future AI tools that aim to augment, rather than automate human creativity.
